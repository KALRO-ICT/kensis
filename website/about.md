# About the Kenya Soil Survey

The **Kenya Soil Survey (KSS)** is a national centre of excellence dedicated to the generation, analysis, and dissemination of soil and land resources information. As a department within the **Kenya Agricultural and Livestock Research Organization (KALRO)**, KSS plays a central role in supporting sustainable agriculture, natural resource management, and environmental stewardship across Kenya.

### **Early Beginnings (1970s)**
KSS was established in the early 1970s to meet the growing need for detailed soil information for agricultural planning and land development. Early work focused on reconnaissance and semi-detailed surveys across major agricultural regions.

### **Development of National Soil Maps**
Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, KSS produced Kenyaâ€™s foundational soil maps and reports, many of which are still widely referenced. These included soil classification maps, land capability assessments, and agro-ecological studies.

### **Modernization and Digital Soil Mapping**
From the 2000s onward, KSS modernized its operations, integrating digital soil mapping, GIS, remote sensing, and advanced laboratory technologies.  
The digital era enabled the transition from traditional survey methods to modelling-based predictions and national-scale soil data products.

### **Integration into KALRO**
With the formation of the Kenya Agricultural and Livestock Research Organization (KALRO), KSS became a core department supporting national research, policy, and innovation systems.
